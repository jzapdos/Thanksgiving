This is a page all about Thanksgiving!

On this page, we will be talking about the history of thanksgiving, how we celebrate it now, and even what we eat!

Thanksgiving is a holiday with roots in both Native American and European traditions. The most commonly referenced origin is the 1621 feast at Plymouth, where English Pilgrims and the Wampanoag people shared a harvest meal. This event is often considered the "First Thanksgiving," though the Pilgrims were motivated by a desire to give thanks for a successful harvest, which they believed was aided by the Wampanoag's assistance in farming techniques.

Today, Thanksgiving is celebrated primarily in the United States as a time for family and friends to gather and give thanks for their blessings. The holiday is marked by a large meal, typically featuring roast turkey, stuffing, mashed potatoes, cranberry sauce, and pumpkin pie, among other dishes. Many people watch the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade, which has become a tradition since 1924, and enjoy football games, both college and professional.
